where we started

Our history begins in 1866, with the foundation of the Anglo-Swiss Clothing Company. Henri Kellinger develops a breakthrough style in 1867, and in 1905 the company he founded merges with Anglo-Swiss, to form what is now known as Kelly's Fashion. During this period cities grow and railways and steamships bring down commodity costs, spurring international trade in consumer goods.

our process

We use Danish and Swedish modern design as the inspiration and starting off point for the collections. We were particularly drawn to the contrast between linear shapes and more fluid and round design sensibility, which we have explored through use of florals, plaids, and textures and seaming details.

Making a Design

why we are better

We are focused on creating sustainable livelihoods and an alternative to prostitution and poaching in a region of Kenya with the country's highest rates of unemployment. Beyond providing jobs, We regularly implement outreach in the neighboring community. One program is a stitching school and hub where women learn how to sew and can access the resources to become entrepreneurs.

we give back

we give 10% of all sales back to the communities and neighborhoods that could use the most help in Kenya. We provide food, clean water, and better housing than they have right now.

workplace skills

Our workers not only get paid, but they are learning valuable skills that they can take into the job market place. These skills will allow them to be able to move to different areas and still be able to provide for their families.

We have a strong focus on women's empowerment, providing free childcare during work hours and a program for women to make and sell re-usable sanitary pads.
